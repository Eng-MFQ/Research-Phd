# Scholarly References: AI & LLMs in Islam and Fatawa

Here are the links to the papers and articles that were reviewed and referenced in your research paper to enrich the context regarding AI in Islam and Fatawa generation.

*   **IslamicLegalBench: Evaluating LLMs Knowledge and Reasoning of Islamic Law Across 1,200 Years of Islamic Pluralist Legal Traditions**
    *   **Authors:** Ezieddin Elmahjub, Junaid Qadir, Abdullah Mushtaq, Rafay Naeem, Ibrahim Ghaznavi, and Waleed Iqbal (2026).
    *   **Link / DOI:** [arXiv:2602.21226](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21226)
    *   **Summary:** Introduces the first multi-school benchmark designed to assess LLM performance in various areas of Islamic jurisprudence.

*   **FiqhQA: A Benchmark for Evaluating LLMs on Islamic Jurisprudence**
    *   **Authors:** Atif et al. (2025).
    *   **Link:** [ResearchGate Profile (requires search/login for direct PDF)](https://www.researchgate.net/)
    *   **Summary:** A benchmark specifically categorized by the four major Sunni schools of thought focusing on evaluating the accuracy and abstention behavior of LLMs in fiqh.

*   **IslamicEval 2025: The First Shared Task of Capturing LLMs Hallucination in Islamic Content**
    *   **Authors:** Hamdy Mubarak, Rana Malhas, Watheq Mansour, et al. (2025).
    *   **Link:** [ACL Anthology / ArabicNLP 2025](https://aclanthology.org/)
    *   **Summary:** The inaugural shared task scheduled for November 2025 focusing heavily on identification and correction of misquoted verses and AI hallucinations in Islamic domains.

*   **The Danger of Relying on AI for Fatwa Writing: A Caution for Scholars and Muftis**
    *   **Authors:** Whitethread Institute publications (2025).
    *   **Link:** [Whitethread Institute](https://whitethread.org/)
    *   **Summary:** Explores the dangers of substituting human muftis (who rely on *ijtihad* and understand context/local nuances) with AI systems that lack moral agency and often hallucinate.
