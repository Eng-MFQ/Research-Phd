\begin{abstract}
% skip one line to make the abstract start with indent
This paper presents a novel approach to automating learner support and question-answering on the topic of Islam and Muslim prayer. We integrated an LLM-based agent to classify incoming user queries into two categories: simple straightforward questions with a known answer and more complex question requiring research and elaboration from a human expert. Our central hypothesis is that we can answer the first category of simple questions automatically, while more complex questions can be redirected to human experts for review, to increase the efficiency without sacrificing the authenticity. This hybrid model balances automation with human expertise, preserving the integrity of religious answers. We integrated the proposed system together with a RAG knowledge-base into a popular e-learning application to validate the approach on real data. We implemented three version of the automation algorithm and evaluated them on the dataset of 98 real-user conversations. The algorithm precision varied from 70 to 82 percent between the three versions, indicating that question extraction and knowledge base quality play a critical role in the overall performance of the proposed system.
\end{abstract}
